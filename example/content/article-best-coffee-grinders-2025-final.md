## Is a burr grinder worth it?

Yes. A burr grinder crushes beans between two surfaces at a consistent size. A blade grinder chops randomly — some powder, some chunks. That inconsistency means some grounds over-extract (bitter) while others under-extract (sour) in the same cup.

We tested the same beans in a blade grinder and the Baratza Encore. The blade grinder produced a muddy, uneven cup. The burr grinder produced a clean, balanced one. Same beans, same water, same method. The grinder was the only variable.

You don't need to spend $200. Even a $90 burr grinder (like the OXO) produces dramatically better results than any blade grinder at any price.

## What to look for when buying a coffee grinder

### Burr type: flat vs conical

Conical burrs are standard in home grinders. They're quieter and produce less heat. Flat burrs give more uniform grinds but cost more. For home use, conical is the right choice unless you're deep into espresso.

### Grind settings

More isn't always better. The Baratza's 40 settings are more than enough. What matters is that the steps between settings are small enough for your brew method. Espresso needs micro-adjustments. French press doesn't.

### Noise

If you grind before the household wakes up, noise matters. Hand grinders are quietest. Among electric grinders, the Eureka Mignon is the quietest we tested.

### Retention

Some grinders hold old grounds in the chamber. This means stale coffee mixing with fresh. Look for low-retention designs, especially for single-dosing.

## FAQ

### How much should I spend on a coffee grinder?

For most home brewers, $90-170 gets you a grinder that'll last years and make noticeably better coffee. Spending more than $200 only makes sense if you're brewing espresso daily.

### Manual vs electric grinder — which is better?

Electric for convenience, manual for portability and grind quality per dollar. If you make 1-2 cups at home, electric saves time. If you travel or want quiet morning grinding, go manual.

### How often should I clean my coffee grinder?

We clean ours weekly — a quick brush of the burrs and chamber. Deep clean with grinder cleaning tablets monthly. It takes 5 minutes and prevents stale oil buildup.

### Can I use a coffee grinder for spices?

Don't. Coffee oils linger in burrs and will flavor your spices (and vice versa). If you need a spice grinder, get a separate blade grinder for that.
