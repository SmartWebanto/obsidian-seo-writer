# Audience

## Primary Segment

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Company type / Demographics | Individual consumers, 25-45, urban/suburban |
| Size / Budget | Willing to spend $50-300 on gear, $15-25/bag on beans |
| Geography | US primarily, English-speaking markets |
| Where they are online | Reddit r/coffee, YouTube, Google, Instagram |

### Buyer Persona

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Title | The Home Upgrader |
| Age / Seniority | 28-40, working professional |
| Budget authority | Personal spending, treats coffee as affordable daily luxury |
| Motivation | Tired of bad office coffee, wants cafe-quality at home without the learning curve |
| Buying trigger | Reads a comparison article, sees clear "best for your situation" recommendation |

### User Persona

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Daily work | Makes 1-2 cups at home before work, weekend pour-over ritual |
| Current pain | Overwhelmed by options, doesn't know what actually matters vs marketing hype |
| Value prop | We cut through the noise with tested, honest recommendations |

### Pain Intensity: 3

## Secondary Segment

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Pain | Gift buyers who want to get "the right one" for a coffee-loving partner/parent |
| Pain intensity | 2 |
| Value prop | Gift guides organized by price, use case, and experience level |

## Disqualification

| Signal | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Professional baristas | Our content is too basic for them |
| Price-only shoppers | We don't recommend the cheapest option if it's bad |

## Content format preferences

| Segment | Format | Engagement |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Home Upgrader | How-to guides, comparison tables, "best X for Y" | High |
| Gift buyer | Gift guides, quick picks, "under $100" lists | Medium |

## Channel preferences — Web / SEO

| Segment | Priority | Strategy |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Home Upgrader | High | Long-form comparison + how-to content |
| Gift buyer | Medium | Seasonal gift guides, holiday content |
