# Example: Brew & Bean (fictional coffee roaster)

This folder shows what the vault looks like after onboarding a brand and running the article pipeline once.

## What's here

```
example/
├── workspace/           Filled context files for "Brew & Bean"
│   ├── BRAND.md         Identity, USP, voice attributes
│   ├── AUDIENCE.md      Primary persona: home coffee enthusiast
│   ├── TOV.md           Warm, knowledgeable, never preachy
│   ├── PILLARS.md       3 pillars: brewing, beans, equipment
│   └── KEYWORDS.md      Keyword clusters per pillar
│
├── research/            Output of Steps 1-6
│   └── article-best-coffee-grinders-2025-research.md
│
├── content/             Output of Steps 7-12
│   └── article-best-coffee-grinders-2025-final.md
│
└── briefs/              Obsidian brief note
    └── article-best-coffee-grinders-2025.md
```

## How context shaped the article

Without the workspace, an article about "best coffee grinders 2025" would be generic buying advice. With it:

- **BRAND.md** → the article references Brew & Bean's own grinder recommendations
- **TOV.md** → warm, first-person "we" voice — not a detached review site tone
- **AUDIENCE.md** → targets home enthusiasts, not baristas — language and depth adjusted
- **PILLARS.md** → article mapped to "Equipment" pillar, connects to other equipment content
- **KEYWORDS.md** → secondary keywords woven in naturally, cannibalization checked

This is one article. Over 20 articles, the consistency compounds.
